Casa Don Bosco

This house has a modernist style and was built at the beginning of the 20th century by the architect from Ronda Santiago Sanguinetti. It originally belonged to a family from Granada that then donated it to the Salesian Congregation in 1940, so that it would be turned into a house of health and rest for the old and ill priests of the Salesians. The house is filled with interesting furniture and features, but the gardens provide the biggest attraction with wonderful views of the Puente Nuevo, gorge and mountains and valleys that surround Ronda.
